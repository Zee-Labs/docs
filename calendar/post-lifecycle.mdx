Every post in Postzee follows a lifecycle from creation to publication. Understanding these statuses helps you manage your content pipeline and troubleshoot issues.

## Post statuses

A post progresses through these statuses:

```
Draft → Scheduled → Queue → Published
                         └→ Error
```

| Status | Color | Description |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| **Draft** | Gray | Post is saved but not scheduled. It won't be published until you schedule it. |
| **Scheduled** | Blue | Post is set to publish at a specific date/time. Waiting for the scheduled moment. |
| **Queue** | Blue | Scheduled time has arrived. The post is being processed and sent to the platform's API. |
| **Published** | Purple | Post was successfully delivered to the platform. It's live. |
| **Error** | Red | Post failed to publish. Check the error details for the reason. |

## Status transitions

### Draft to Scheduled

When you set a date/time and click **Schedule**, the post moves from Draft to Scheduled. It appears on your Calendar with a blue indicator.

You can still edit the content and reschedule while in this status.

### Scheduled to Queue

At the scheduled time, the post automatically enters the Queue. Postzee's background workers pick it up and begin the publishing process.

This transition is automatic — no action needed from you.

### Queue to Published

The worker sends your content to the platform's API (Instagram, TikTok, LinkedIn, etc.). On success, the post moves to Published and the purple indicator appears on your Calendar.

### Queue to Error

If the platform's API rejects the post or a connection issue occurs, the post moves to Error status. The specific error reason is saved so you can diagnose the problem.

## What you can do at each status

| Action | Draft | Scheduled | Published | Error |
|--------|:---:|:---:|:---:|:---:|
| Edit content | Yes | Yes | — | — |
| Reschedule | Yes | Yes | — | — |
| Drag on calendar | Yes | Yes | — | — |
| Delete | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Retry | — | — | — | Yes |
| View details | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |

## Handling errors

When a post fails, here's how to fix it:

1. Find the post on your Calendar (it has a red indicator)
2. Click on it to see the **error details**
3. Fix the underlying issue based on the error reason
4. Click **Retry** to attempt publishing again

### Common error reasons

| Error | Cause | Solution |
|-------|-------|----------|
| **Channel disconnected** | OAuth token expired or revoked | Go to **Channels** → reconnect the account |
| **Channel disabled** | The channel was manually disabled | Go to **Channels** → enable the channel |
| **Content rejected** | Platform rejected the content (format, size, policy) | Edit the post to match the platform's requirements |
| **Rate limited** | Too many posts sent to the platform in a short time | Wait a few minutes and retry |
| **Account blocked** | Your Postzee account has a billing or policy issue | Check your account status in **Settings** |

## Multi-channel post behavior

When you publish to multiple channels simultaneously:

- Each channel is processed independently
- If one channel fails, the others still publish successfully
- Failed channels show an Error status — you can retry them individually
- All posts in the same group share a **group ID** linking them together

### Example

You schedule a post for Instagram, LinkedIn, and X (Twitter) at 10:00 AM:

| Channel | Result | Status |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Instagram | Delivered successfully | Published |
| LinkedIn | Delivered successfully | Published |
| X (Twitter) | Token expired | Error |

In this case, you only need to reconnect your X (Twitter) account and retry that single post. Instagram and LinkedIn are already live.

## Deletion

When you delete a post:

- **Draft / Scheduled** — the post is removed from your Calendar. Nothing was published.
- **Published** — the post is removed from Postzee's Calendar, but the content **remains live on the platform**. To remove it from the platform, you need to delete it directly from Instagram, LinkedIn, etc.
- **Error** — the post is removed. Since it was never published, there's nothing to clean up.

<Note>
Deleting a post in Postzee does not delete it from the social media platform. If a post was already published, you must remove it directly from the platform.
</Note>

## Post comments

Team members can leave comments on any post for collaboration:

- Open a post from the Calendar
- Add a comment in the comment section
- Comments are visible to all team members
