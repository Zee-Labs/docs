---
title: Scheduling Posts
icon: clock
---

Schedule your posts to publish automatically at the perfect time. Postzee handles timezone conversion, queue management, and delivery to all your connected channels.

## Publishing options

When creating or editing a post, you have three publishing options:

| Option | What it does |
|--------|-------------|
| **Publish now** | Sends the post immediately to your selected channels |
| **Schedule** | Queues the post to publish at a specific date and time |
| **Save as Draft** | Saves the post without scheduling — you can schedule it later |

## How to schedule a post

1. Open the **Post Editor** (from Calendar, Zee Studio, or the **Create Post** button)
2. Write your content and select your channels
3. Pick a date and time using the **date/time picker**
4. Click **Schedule**
5. The post appears on your Calendar with a blue **Scheduled** indicator
6. At the scheduled time, Postzee automatically publishes to all selected channels

<Note>
The minimum scheduling time is the current moment. You cannot schedule posts in the past.
</Note>

## Timezone handling

All scheduled times respect your team's configured timezone:

- The date/time picker shows times in **your timezone**
- When publishing, Postzee converts to UTC internally and delivers at the exact moment you selected
- Team members in different timezones all see the same local time relative to the team's setting

To change your team's timezone, open the account drawer → **Settings** → **Preferences** tab → **Timezone**.

## Find the best time

Not sure when to post? Use the **Find Best Time** feature:

1. In the post editor, click **Find Best Time** (or the clock icon)
2. Postzee analyzes your calendar and finds the least congested time slots
3. A suggested date/time is automatically filled in
4. Adjust if needed, then click **Schedule**

The algorithm looks at your existing scheduled posts across all channels and suggests times when your calendar is least busy, spreading your content evenly throughout the day.

## Rescheduling

There are two ways to reschedule a post:

### Drag and drop (Calendar)

1. Open the **Calendar** view (Month, Week, or Day)
2. Click and drag the post to a new date or time slot
3. The post is instantly rescheduled to the new time

### Edit the post

1. Click on the post in the Calendar or Posts list
2. Change the date/time in the editor
3. Click **Save** or **Schedule**

<Note>
Only **Draft** and **Scheduled** posts can be rescheduled. Once a post is published, its date is locked.
</Note>

## Recurring posts

Set up posts that automatically repeat on a regular interval:

1. Create your post and schedule the first occurrence
2. Set the **Repeat interval** (in days) — for example, every 7 days for weekly
3. Click **Schedule**
4. Postzee automatically creates future occurrences at the specified interval

Recurring posts are grouped together on the calendar with matching color indicators. You can manage the entire series or edit individual occurrences.

## Multi-channel scheduling

When you select multiple channels, the same post is scheduled for all of them simultaneously:

- All channels receive the post at the same scheduled time
- Platform-specific settings (TikTok privacy, YouTube visibility, etc.) are applied per channel
- If one channel fails, the others still publish successfully — you can retry the failed one individually

## Queue processing

Here's what happens behind the scenes when your scheduled time arrives:

1. The post moves from **Scheduled** to **Queue** status
2. Postzee's worker processes the queue and sends content to each platform's API
3. On success, the post moves to **Published** status
4. On failure, the post moves to **Error** status with a specific error message
