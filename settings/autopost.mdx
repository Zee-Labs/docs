Autopost lets you automatically import content from RSS feeds and publish it to your social channels. Connect a blog, podcast, or any RSS-compatible source, and Postzee will create posts whenever new items are detected.

## How to access

Open the account drawer (click your avatar in the top-right) → **Settings** → **Autopost** tab.

## What is Autopost?

Autopost monitors RSS feeds you configure and automatically creates posts in Postzee whenever a new item is published in the feed. You decide which channels each feed publishes to and how the posts are formatted.

Common use cases:

- **Auto-share blog posts** to all your social channels when published
- **Cross-post podcast episodes** to LinkedIn and X
- **Distribute news** from external feeds to your community
- **Republish your own content** from one platform to another

## How to configure

1. Go to **Settings** → **Autopost**
2. Click **Add Feed**
3. Enter:
   - **RSS URL** — the feed to monitor
   - **Channels** — which channels to publish to
   - **Post template** — how to format the post (e.g., title + link, or custom text)
   - **Check interval** — how often Postzee checks the feed for new items
4. Click **Save**

## Post templates

Use placeholders to dynamically build posts from feed items:

| Placeholder | Replaced with |
|-------------|--------------|
| `{title}` | The title of the feed item |
| `{description}` | The item description/summary |
| `{link}` | The URL of the item |
| `{author}` | The item's author |
| `{pubDate}` | The publication date |

### Example template

```
🚀 New post: {title}

{description}

Read more: {link}
```

## Check intervals

Configure how often Postzee polls the feed:

- **15 minutes** — for high-frequency feeds (news, breaking content)
- **1 hour** — for typical blogs (recommended default)
- **6 hours** — for low-frequency feeds (weekly newsletters, podcasts)
- **24 hours** — for very slow feeds

More frequent checks consume more resources but detect new content faster.

## Filtering items

You can filter which items are autoposted:

- **Keyword filters** — only post items matching specific keywords
- **Category filters** — only post items in specific feed categories
- **Date filters** — ignore items older than X days when first connecting a feed

## Per-channel settings

Each feed can publish to multiple channels with different settings. For example:

- LinkedIn: full post with description
- X (Twitter): truncated to 280 chars
- Instagram: title only with image from feed

## Plan availability

Autopost is available on **Team**, **Pro**, and **Ultimate** plans.

<Note>
Autopost uses your monthly post quota. Each item published from a feed counts as one post, so a feed with 30 new items per month uses 30 of your post slots.
</Note>

## Disabling a feed

To stop autoposting:

1. Open the feed in **Settings** → **Autopost**
2. Toggle **Active** to off (or delete the feed entirely)
3. Posts already in the queue are still published, but no new items will be detected
