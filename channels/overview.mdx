**Channels** are your connected social media accounts. Each channel represents one profile, page, or account where Postzee can publish content on your behalf.

## What is a channel?

A channel is a linked social media account — for example, your Instagram business profile, your LinkedIn company page, or your TikTok creator account. Once connected, you can schedule and publish posts to that channel directly from Postzee.

## How to access

Click **Channels** in the top navigation bar. You'll see all your connected channels with their status, platform, and profile information.

## Channel information

Each channel card displays:

| Field | Description |
|-------|-------------|
| **Profile picture** | The account's avatar from the platform |
| **Name** | The display name of the account |
| **Platform** | Which social network (Instagram, LinkedIn, etc.) |
| **Status** | Enabled, Disabled, or Needs Reconnection |

## Channel limits by plan

The number of channels you can connect depends on your plan:

| Plan | Channels |
|------|----------|
| **Free** | 0 (AI features only) |
| **Standard** | 5 |
| **Team** | 10 |
| **Pro** | 30 |
| **Ultimate** | 100 |

Each connection counts as one channel. For example, connecting 2 Instagram accounts and 1 LinkedIn page uses 3 of your channel slots.

## Managing channels

### Enable / Disable

Toggle a channel on or off without removing it. Disabled channels:
- Don't appear in the post editor's channel selector
- Keep their connection and settings intact
- Can be re-enabled at any time

### Reconnect

If a channel shows **Needs Reconnection**, the OAuth token has expired or been revoked. Click **Reconnect** to re-authorize through the platform's login flow.

### Delete

Remove a channel permanently. This disconnects the account and removes it from your channel list. Existing published posts are not affected.

<Note>
Deleting a channel from Postzee does not delete your social media account or any posts already published to it.
</Note>
