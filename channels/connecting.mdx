Connect your social media accounts to Postzee so you can publish content from one dashboard. Each platform uses a secure OAuth authorization flow — Postzee never sees or stores your password.

## How to connect a channel

1. Go to **Channels** in the left sidebar
2. Click **Add Channel**
3. Select the platform you want to connect (e.g., Instagram, LinkedIn, TikTok)
4. You'll be redirected to the platform's login page
5. Log in and **authorize** Postzee to access your account
6. You're redirected back to Postzee — your channel is now connected

<Note>
Some platforms (like Instagram and Facebook) share the same authorization flow via Facebook Business. Connecting one may give you access to connect the other.
</Note>

## Platform-specific instructions

### Instagram

1. Click **Add Channel** → **Instagram**
2. Log in with your **Facebook** account (Instagram business profiles are managed through Facebook)
3. Select the Instagram Business or Creator profile you want to connect
4. Authorize the required permissions
5. Your Instagram channel appears in the list

<Note>
Instagram requires a **Business** or **Creator** profile connected to a Facebook Page. Personal Instagram accounts cannot be connected via the API.
</Note>

### Facebook

1. Click **Add Channel** → **Facebook**
2. Log in with your Facebook account
3. Select the **Page** or **Group** you want to manage
4. Authorize permissions
5. Your Facebook channel is ready

### TikTok

1. Click **Add Channel** → **TikTok**
2. Log in with your TikTok account
3. Authorize Postzee
4. Configure content disclosure settings (required by TikTok's API)
5. Your TikTok channel is connected

### LinkedIn

1. Click **Add Channel** → **LinkedIn**
2. Log in with your LinkedIn account
3. Choose between your **Personal Profile** or a **Company Page**
4. Authorize permissions
5. Your LinkedIn channel is ready

### YouTube

1. Click **Add Channel** → **YouTube**
2. Log in with your Google account
3. Select the YouTube channel you want to manage
4. Authorize permissions
5. Your YouTube channel is connected

### X (Twitter)

1. Click **Add Channel** → **X (Twitter)**
2. Log in with your X account
3. Authorize Postzee
4. Your X channel is ready

### Other platforms

For platforms like Pinterest, Threads, Bluesky, Discord, Telegram, Slack, Reddit, Mastodon, and others — the flow is similar:

1. Click **Add Channel** → select the platform
2. Complete the platform's authorization or provide the required credentials
3. The channel appears in your list

Some platforms (Discord, Telegram, Slack) use **webhook-based** connections instead of OAuth — you'll be guided through the setup.

For self-hosted platforms like **Mastodon** or **WordPress**, you'll need to provide your instance URL during setup.

## Multiple accounts

You can connect multiple accounts from the same platform. For example:

- 2 Instagram Business profiles
- 3 LinkedIn Company Pages
- 1 TikTok account

Each connection counts as one channel toward your plan's limit.

## Reconnecting a channel

OAuth tokens can expire or be revoked. When this happens, the channel shows a **Needs Reconnection** indicator.

To fix it:

1. Go to **Channels**
2. Find the channel with the reconnection warning
3. Click **Reconnect**
4. Complete the OAuth flow again
5. The channel is restored with all its settings intact

<Note>
Scheduled posts for a disconnected channel will fail when their publish time arrives. Reconnect the channel before the scheduled time to avoid errors.
</Note>

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Authorization fails | Make sure you're logging into the correct account on the platform |
| Instagram not showing | Ensure your Instagram is a Business or Creator profile linked to a Facebook Page |
| Channel shows "Needs Reconnection" | The token expired — click Reconnect to re-authorize |
| Platform not in the list | Check [Supported Platforms](/get-started/supported-platforms) for the full list, or contact support |
