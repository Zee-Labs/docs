Connect your Instagram Business or Creator profile to Postzee to publish photos, carousels, Reels, and Stories directly from the dashboard.

## Requirements

- An **Instagram Business** or **Creator** profile (personal accounts are not supported by the API)
- A **Facebook Page** linked to your Instagram profile
- A **Facebook account** with admin access to that Page

<Note>
Instagram's API is managed through Facebook. You'll log in with your Facebook account during the connection process.
</Note>

## How to connect

1. Go to **Channels** in the top navigation bar
2. Click **Add Channel** → **Instagram**
3. Log in with your **Facebook** account
4. Select the Facebook Page linked to your Instagram profile
5. Authorize the required permissions
6. Your Instagram channel appears in the channel list

## Supported content types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **Photo** | Single image post to your feed |
| **Carousel** | Multiple images in a swipeable post (up to 10 images) |
| **Reel** | Short-form video (vertical, up to 90 seconds) |
| **Story** | Temporary content that disappears after 24 hours |

## Platform-specific settings

When creating a post for Instagram, you can configure:

| Setting | Options |
|---------|---------|
| **Post Type** | Feed Post or Story |
| **Collaborators** | Tag other Instagram accounts as collaborators |

## Tips

- **Aspect ratios:** Square (1:1) and portrait (4:5) work best for feed posts. Use 9:16 for Reels and Stories.
- **Hashtags:** Add relevant hashtags in your caption for discovery (up to 30, but 5-10 targeted hashtags often perform best).
- **Carousel posts:** Great for step-by-step content, before/after comparisons, or telling a visual story.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---------|----------|
| Instagram not showing during connection | Ensure your Instagram is a Business or Creator profile linked to a Facebook Page |
| "Permissions error" | Make sure your Facebook account has admin access to the linked Page |
| Posts failing | Check that your media meets Instagram's format requirements (JPEG/PNG for images, MP4 for Reels) |
