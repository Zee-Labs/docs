Beyond the major social networks, Postzee supports 20+ additional platforms for publishing content. Here's a quick reference for each.

## Messaging & Communication

### Telegram

- **Connection:** Bot token or channel link
- **Content:** Messages, images, videos
- **Use case:** Channel updates, group notifications

### Discord

- **Connection:** Webhook URL
- **Content:** Messages, images
- **Use case:** Server announcements, community updates

### Slack

- **Connection:** Webhook or OAuth
- **Content:** Messages
- **Use case:** Team notifications, content updates

## Community & Forums

### Reddit

- **Connection:** OAuth
- **Content:** Posts, images
- **Use case:** Subreddit content, community engagement

### Mastodon

- **Connection:** OAuth (requires instance URL)
- **Content:** Posts, images
- **Use case:** Fediverse publishing, decentralized social media

<Note>
Mastodon is self-hosted — you'll need to provide your instance URL (e.g., `mastodon.social`) during connection.
</Note>

### Lemmy

- **Connection:** OAuth
- **Content:** Posts
- **Use case:** Fediverse community platform

### Warpcast

- **Connection:** OAuth
- **Content:** Posts
- **Use case:** Farcaster protocol publishing

### Nostr

- **Connection:** Protocol key
- **Content:** Posts
- **Use case:** Decentralized social publishing

### VK

- **Connection:** OAuth
- **Content:** Posts, images
- **Use case:** CIS region social network

## Blogging & Publishing

### Medium

- **Connection:** OAuth
- **Content:** Articles
- **Use case:** Cross-posting blog content

### Dev.to

- **Connection:** API key
- **Content:** Articles
- **Use case:** Developer community publishing

### Hashnode

- **Connection:** API key
- **Content:** Articles
- **Use case:** Developer blogging

### WordPress

- **Connection:** Application password (requires site URL)
- **Content:** Posts
- **Use case:** Blog publishing, CMS integration

### ListMonk

- **Connection:** API credentials
- **Content:** Newsletters
- **Use case:** Email marketing campaigns

## Portfolio & Professional

### Dribbble

- **Connection:** OAuth
- **Content:** Design shots
- **Use case:** Designer portfolio publishing

### Google Business Profile

- **Connection:** OAuth
- **Content:** Business updates
- **Use case:** Local business listing updates

## How to connect any platform

The process is similar for all platforms:

1. Go to **Channels** in the top navigation bar
2. Click **Add Channel**
3. Select the platform
4. Complete the authorization (OAuth, API key, or webhook depending on the platform)
5. The channel appears in your list

<Note>
Self-hosted platforms (Mastodon, WordPress, Lemmy) require you to provide your instance URL during setup. Webhook-based platforms (Discord, Slack, Telegram) require you to configure a webhook URL.
</Note>
