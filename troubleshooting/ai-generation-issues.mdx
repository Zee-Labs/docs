## My AI generation failed — where are my credits?

**Credits are automatically refunded** when a generation fails. You don't need to request a refund — it happens instantly.

Check your credit balance by clicking your **profile picture** (top right) → **Credits**. The refunded amount matches exactly what was deducted for that generation.

<Note>
You'll receive a notification when a generation fails. The notification includes the reason for the failure (content blocked, timeout, etc.).
</Note>

## Common failure reasons

### Content blocked

**Cause:** The AI model's safety filter detected potentially inappropriate content in your prompt.

**Fix:**
- Rephrase your prompt to avoid sensitive topics
- Avoid requests for realistic depictions of real people
- Remove explicit or violent descriptions
- Try a different AI model — each model has different content policies

### Generation timed out

**Cause:** The AI model took too long to generate. This is more common with video models and high-resolution images.

**Fix:**
- Try again — timeouts are usually temporary server load issues
- For videos: try a shorter duration (e.g., 5 seconds instead of 10)
- For images: try a lower resolution or different aspect ratio
- Try during off-peak hours

### Image reference issues

**Cause:** The reference image you provided couldn't be processed — it may be too small, too large, or in an unsupported format.

**Fix:**
- Use images between 512×512 and 4096×4096 pixels
- Use JPG, PNG, or WebP format
- Ensure the image file is not corrupted
- Try without the reference image to isolate the issue

### No output generated

**Cause:** The AI model couldn't produce a result from your prompt. This can happen with very abstract or contradictory prompts.

**Fix:**
- Be more specific in your description
- Use the **Prompt Optimizer** (✨ button) to improve your prompt
- Try a different model that may interpret your prompt better

## My image quality is poor

**Tips for better results:**

1. **Use the Prompt Optimizer** — Click the ✨ button to let AI enhance your prompt. This dramatically improves results.

2. **Be specific** — "A golden retriever puppy on a beach at sunset, professional photography, shallow depth of field" works better than "dog on beach."

3. **Choose the right model:**
   - **Photorealism:** Nano Banana Pro, FLUX.2 Pro, GPT Image 2 (HD quality)
   - **Illustrations/Art:** Recraft V4, Ideogram V3
   - **Fast drafts:** GPT Image Mini, Nano Banana
   - **Text in images:** Ideogram V3 (best at rendering text accurately)

4. **Aspect ratio matters** — Use the aspect ratio that matches your target platform (9:16 for Stories/Reels, 1:1 for feed posts, 16:9 for YouTube).

## My video has visual artifacts

**Common causes:**
- Very fast motion in the prompt — AI video models struggle with rapid movement
- Very detailed scenes with many small elements
- Unrealistic physics requests

**Fix:**
- Simplify the scene description
- Use prompts that describe slow, smooth motion
- Try a different video model (each has different strengths)
- For best quality, use **Kling 2.0 Master** or **Veo 3.1**

## The AI generated wrong text in the image

**Cause:** Most AI models struggle with text rendering. They may misspell words or produce garbled text.

**Best options for text in images:**
1. Use **Ideogram V3** — it's specifically designed for accurate text rendering
2. Use **Recraft V4** — excellent for logos and design with text
3. Generate the image without text, then add text using the **Photo Editor**

## I ran out of credits

**Options to get more credits:**
1. **Purchase credit packages** — Click your **profile picture** (top right) → **Credits** → choose a package
2. **Wait for monthly reset** — Paid plans receive fresh credits each billing cycle
3. **Enable Auto-Recharge** — Click your **profile picture** → **Credits** → **Auto-Recharge** tab, set a threshold to automatically purchase more credits when your balance gets low

<Note>
Monthly plan credits reset at each billing cycle. Purchased credits never expire, even if you cancel your subscription.
</Note>
