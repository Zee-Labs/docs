When a post fails to publish, it shows an **Error** status in the calendar. Here are the most common causes and how to fix them.

## My post shows "Error" status

1. Open the **Calendar**
2. Click on the post with the error icon
3. Click **View Error** to see the error message
4. Follow the specific fix below based on the error

## Channel token expired

**Symptoms:** Post fails with an authorization or token error.

**Cause:** OAuth tokens from social platforms expire periodically (typically every 60 days for Facebook/Instagram). When the token expires, Postzee can no longer publish on your behalf.

**Fix:**
1. Go to **Channels**
2. Find the channel showing "Needs Reconnection"
3. Click **Reconnect**
4. Complete the authorization flow
5. Reschedule the failed post

<Note>
Postzee sends you a notification when a channel needs reconnection. Reconnect it before your next scheduled post to avoid errors.
</Note>

## Content rejected by platform

**Symptoms:** Post fails with a content policy or moderation error.

**Cause:** The social platform (Instagram, TikTok, etc.) detected that the content violates their community guidelines.

**Fix:**
1. Review the error message for specific details
2. Edit the post content — remove any flagged text or media
3. Reschedule the post

Common reasons for rejection:
- Explicit or sensitive content
- Copyrighted music in videos
- Spam-like text or excessive hashtags
- Text overlays that cover too much of the image (Instagram)

## Unsupported media format

**Symptoms:** Post fails with a format or file error.

**Cause:** The media file format is not supported by the platform.

**Fix:**
- **Videos:** Must be in **MP4** format. Convert other formats (MOV, AVI, MKV) to MP4 before uploading.
- **Images:** Use **JPG, PNG, or WebP**. Most platforms don't accept BMP, TIFF, or SVG.
- Check platform-specific limits (e.g., TikTok requires videos between 3-60 seconds)

## Post published but shows as error

**Symptoms:** The post actually appeared on the social network, but Postzee shows it as "Error."

**Cause:** The platform accepted the post but returned an unexpected response format. This is rare and usually caused by temporary API issues.

**Fix:**
- Check the social network directly to confirm the post is live
- The post will remain as "Error" in Postzee, but no action is needed
- If this happens frequently, contact support

## Rate limit exceeded

**Symptoms:** Multiple posts fail in a short time, error mentions "rate limit" or "too many requests."

**Cause:** You're publishing too many posts in a short period. Each platform has limits on how many posts can be made per hour/day.

**Fix:**
- Space out your scheduled posts (at least 5-10 minutes apart)
- Reduce the number of posts per day to that platform
- Wait 1-2 hours and reschedule the failed posts

## Post was scheduled but never published

**Symptoms:** The post stays in "Scheduled" status even after the publish time has passed.

**Cause:** The channel was disconnected or disabled when the publish time arrived.

**Fix:**
1. Check if the channel is connected and active in **Channels**
2. Reconnect if needed
3. Reschedule the post to a future time

<Warning>
Posts cannot be published retroactively. If a scheduled post missed its window, you must reschedule it to a new date/time.
</Warning>

## Still having issues?

If none of the above solutions work:
1. Check the error message carefully — it often includes specific guidance
2. Try disconnecting and reconnecting the affected channel
3. Contact support through the chat widget with the error message
