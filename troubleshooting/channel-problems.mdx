## My channel shows "Needs Reconnection"

**Cause:** OAuth tokens expire periodically (usually every 60 days for Facebook/Instagram). When this happens, Postzee can no longer publish on your behalf.

**Fix:**
1. Go to **Channels**
2. Find the channel with the warning indicator
3. Click the **⋮** menu → **Reconnect**
4. Complete the OAuth authorization flow
5. Your channel is restored with all settings intact

<Note>
Scheduled posts for a disconnected channel will fail when their publish time arrives. Reconnect before the scheduled time to avoid errors.
</Note>

## Reconnection failed

**Cause:** The platform may require a completely new connection instead of a reconnection. This can happen when the platform revokes your previous authorization entirely.

**Fix:**
1. Go to **Channels**
2. **Remove** the problematic channel (click ⋮ → Remove)
3. Click **Add Channel** and connect it again from scratch
4. Reassign any scheduled posts to the new channel

## I can't connect my Instagram

**Common causes:**

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Instagram not showing up | Your Instagram must be a **Business** or **Creator** profile. Personal profiles cannot be connected via the API. |
| No pages appear after login | Your Instagram Business profile must be linked to a **Facebook Page**. Go to Instagram Settings → Account → Linked Accounts → Facebook. |
| Authorization error | Make sure you're logging in with the **Facebook account** that manages the Instagram profile, not the Instagram credentials directly. |
| "Insufficient permissions" | During authorization, make sure you check **all** the permission boxes that Postzee requests. |

## I can't connect my Facebook Page

**Common causes:**

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| No pages appear | You must be an **Admin** of the Facebook Page. Editor or Moderator roles are not sufficient. |
| Wrong page connected | You can connect multiple pages. If you connected the wrong one, remove it and add the correct one. |
| "Page not accessible" | Ensure the page is published (not unpublished/draft) and you have admin access. |

## I can't connect TikTok

**Common causes:**

| Issue | Solution |
|-------|----------|
| Authorization fails | Make sure you're using a **TikTok Business** or **Creator** account. Personal accounts have limited API access. |
| Content disclosure required | TikTok requires content disclosure settings. Postzee will prompt you to configure these during connection. |

## I connected the wrong account

1. Go to **Channels**
2. Find the incorrect channel
3. Click ⋮ → **Remove**
4. Click **Add Channel** and connect the correct account

Removing a channel does **not** delete any posts already published on the platform. It only disconnects Postzee's access.

## My channel appears disabled

**Cause:** Your plan's channel limit was exceeded, or an admin disabled the channel.

**Fix:**
1. Check your plan's channel limit in **Settings** → **Billing**
2. If you've exceeded your limit, upgrade your plan or remove unused channels
3. If an admin disabled it, ask them to re-enable it in **Channels** → ⋮ → **Enable**

## Channel limit reached

Each plan has a maximum number of channels:

| Plan | Max Channels |
|------|-------------|
| Free | 0 |
| Standard | 5 |
| Team | 10 |
| Pro | 30 |
| Ultimate | 100 |

To connect more channels, upgrade your plan in **Settings** → **Billing**.
